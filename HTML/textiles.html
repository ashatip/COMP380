E-Textiles

The recent history of electronically enhanced fabrics

What are E-Textiles?

E-textiles refers to any fabric which enable electronic/digital components to be embedded in them. They accomplish this through the use of conductive fabrics made in part by malleable transition metals such as copper, and gold, which are then weaved into clothing for practical or stylistic reasons. While e-textiles are a recently emerging technology, the practice of weaving conductive metals into fabric has existed since at least the 16th century, paving the way for modern techniques many years before hand. Today's researchers interest themselves with the medical applications, as well as the fashion design implications of e-textiles, of which many still remain unknown.
