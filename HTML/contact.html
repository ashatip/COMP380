Studies & Research

This site's goal is to provide you with unbiased information regarding wearable technology. Effect of Wearable Technology Combined With a Lifestyle Intervention on Long-term Weight Loss The IDEA Randomized Clinical Trial presents findings from a behavioral weight loss program with two groups of semi-equal overweight and obese people. The group using wearable technology lost less weight than the group that did not. However, it is important to note that critics such as Doctor Doina Kulick and Doctor Corrine I. Voils negatively reviewed the methodology of the studies that have been completed and consequently the results of the study. Additionally, there are a number of other studies that measure the effectiveness of wearable technology. Take the survey to the left to contribute to our own study of wearable technology on this site.

Sources for survey

Additional research like the Harvard Health Publication provides averages of calories burned by individuals from biking, swimming, running, and going to gym based on their weight. The survey results at the bottom of the page were constructed based on the calorie numbers from this study. Averages were based on the following: bicycling at 14-15.9 mph, running at 5.2 mph, swimming laps vigoursly/generally, and weight lifting vigoursly/generally.
